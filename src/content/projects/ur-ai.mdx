## What we're building

AI products focused on **structured workflows** — the long tail of the operational work knowledge workers spend their days on. Document intelligence. Automation. The space between rigid forms and free-form chat, where most actual work lives.

## My role

Technical co-founder. Owning **product, engineering, and system architecture end-to-end** as the CTO. Setting the technical direction, hiring the engineering team, and writing a meaningful share of the production code while we're early.

## The thinking

Most "AI for work" so far has been either too rigid (RPA-style automation that breaks the moment a form changes) or too loose (chat assistants that need a prompt for every action). The interesting space is in between — systems that understand the *structure* of what someone is trying to do and can adapt within it. That's the bet.
